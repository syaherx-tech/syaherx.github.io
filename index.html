Philosophy of Experience

“We never observe causation — only patterns.”

Inspired by David Hume’s idea of causation, what we call “cause and effect” may simply be repeated experience in the mind.

🔥 Fire & Cotton

We say fire burns cotton, but what we truly see is only fire followed by burning — not the hidden force itself.

🧠 Mind

The mind forms habits from repetition and assumes the future will resemble the past.

🌌 Reality

What we call “truth” may be a structure built from repeated perception, not direct access to necessity.
